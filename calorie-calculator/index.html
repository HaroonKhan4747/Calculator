What Your Results Mean

Once you’ve calculated your daily calories, you’ll see three important numbers that guide your nutrition plan.

Maintenance Calories

This is the number of calories your body needs to maintain your current weight. Eating around this number keeps your energy levels stable and your weight steady.

Weight Gain / Loss Options

If your goal is to gain muscle (bulk), you’ll need a calorie surplus — typically 250–500 extra calories per day. To lose fat, you’ll aim for a calorie deficit of 300–500 calories per day. Consistency is key for both goals.

Macros Breakdown (Protein, Carbs, Fats)

Your results also include a macronutrient breakdown. Here’s a simple guide:

  • Protein: 1.6–2.2g per kg of body weight (crucial for muscle repair and growth)
  • Fats: 20–30% of total calories (supports hormones and energy)
  • Carbs: Fill the remaining calories (fuel for workouts and recovery)

How the Calculator Works (Mifflin–St Jeor Formula)

The Mifflin–St Jeor equation is one of the most accurate formulas for estimating calorie needs. It calculates your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) — the calories your body burns at rest — and then multiplies it by an activity factor.

Formula:

  • For men: BMR = (10 × weight in kg) + (6.25 × height in cm) – (5 × age in years) + 5
  • For women: BMR = (10 × weight in kg) + (6.25 × height in cm) – (5 × age in years) – 161

This number is then multiplied by your activity level to give your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) — the total calories you burn in a day.

Estimating Calories Without a Calculator

Don’t have access to a calculator? You can still estimate your calorie needs using simple rules of thumb:

  • For weight loss: 11–13 calories per pound of body weight
  • For maintenance: 14–16 calories per pound
  • For muscle gain: 17–19 calories per pound

Common Questions About Calories

How many calories should I eat to bulk as a skinny guy?

If you’re a beginner or naturally lean, start with a 300–500 calorie surplus. Focus on protein-rich foods, lift heavy, and track your progress weekly.

What’s the best way to lean bulk?

Lean bulking means gaining muscle with minimal fat. Keep your surplus small (around 250–300 calories), eat mostly whole foods, and prioritize strength training.

Can I lose fat and gain muscle at the same time?

Yes, but it’s slower. Focus on eating at maintenance or a slight deficit, getting enough protein, and strength training consistently.

Do calories really matter for muscle growth?

Absolutely. Without enough calories and protein, your body won’t have the energy or nutrients it needs to build muscle — no matter how hard you train.
