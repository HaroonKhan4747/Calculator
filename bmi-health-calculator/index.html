What Is BMI and Why It Matters

BMI, or Body Mass Index, is a numerical value that helps you understand if your weight is healthy relative to your height. While it doesn’t differentiate between fat, muscle, and bone mass, it provides a quick indicator of whether you are underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese. Using a reliable BMI calculator can give you an accurate measurement in seconds.

How to Calculate BMI

Calculating BMI is simple and can be done using either metric or US units:

  • Metric: BMI = weight (kg) ÷ height² (m²)
  • US Units: BMI = (weight (lbs) ÷ height² (in²)) × 703

This method helps answer common questions like “how to calculate BMI formula,” “how to calculate body mass ratio,” or “what is my BMI for my height and weight.”

BMI Prime & Ponderal Index Explained

BMI Prime compares your BMI to the upper limit of normal (usually 25) to show how far you are from a healthy weight. The Ponderal Index uses height cubed instead of squared, which can give a better reading for very tall or short individuals. Both tools are excellent for fine-tuning your weight assessment.
