What Is BMI and Why It Matters

BMI, or Body Mass Index, is a measure that helps you understand if your weight is healthy relative to your height. While it doesn’t account for muscle mass or bone density, it’s a quick way to determine whether you are underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese.

Many search terms target this concept, like “how to calculate BMI of a person” or “calculate BMI and Ponderal Index online”.

How to Calculate BMI

You can calculate BMI using a simple formula:

  • Metric: BMI = weight (kg) ÷ height² (m²)
  • US Units: BMI = (weight (lbs) ÷ height² (in²)) × 703

Popular search queries include “how to calculate BMI formula”, “how to calculate body mass ratio”, and “what is my BMI for my height and weight.”

BMI Prime & Ponderal Index Explained

BMI Prime is a ratio of your BMI to the upper healthy limit (usually 25). It helps identify how far you are from a normal BMI. The Ponderal Index is similar but uses height cubed instead of squared, which is better for very tall or short individuals.

Tips for Achieving a Healthy Weight
