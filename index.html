Frequently Asked Questions (Gulf, 2025)

How much salary do you need to survive in Dubai (UAE) in 2025?

For a single person, survival on a tight budget is possible around 3,000–4,500 AED if you share a room (bedspace), cook at home, and use the metro. A more comfortable solo lifestyle in a studio typically needs 6,000–8,000 AED. If you prefer a 1‑bedroom with a car and frequent dining out, costs can reach 12,000–18,000 AED per month. Use the calculator above to plug in your salary and see itemized rent, food, transport, and other expenses.

Is 4,000 AED enough to live in Dubai?

It’s possible but tight. At around 4,000 AED you’ll likely need a bedspace/shared room, cook most meals at home, and rely on public transport. Expect minimal discretionary spending and little savings. If you can push your income to 5,000–6,000 AED, life becomes more manageable. The Basic preset in the calculator reflects a realistic survival budget.

What is the average rent for a studio in Dubai?

In 2025, many mid‑range areas list studios around 3,000–4,500 AED per month, with location making the biggest difference. Central or waterfront areas cost more; outskirts and older buildings are cheaper. Our Moderate preset uses a realistic mid‑point and includes utilities/Wi‑Fi so you see a complete picture.

What is a good salary in Dubai for a single person?

A common target is 8,000–12,000 AED for a comfortable single lifestyle (studio, some dining out, normal leisure). Higher earners with a car and city‑center living can easily spend more. “Good” also depends on your saving goals—set the calculator to your salary to see expected monthly surplus.

How much does it cost to live in Riyadh (Saudi Arabia) monthly?

Riyadh is generally cheaper than Dubai. A frugal shared setup can be done near 1,300–2,000 SAR, a comfortable solo studio around 3,000–3,700 SAR, and a 1‑bed with car lifestyle around 7,000–9,000 SAR depending on car choice and dining habits. Check the Saudi preset for itemized rent, food, transport, and car costs.

How much do you need to live in Doha (Qatar)?

Doha costs are closer to Dubai than Riyadh. A basic shared arrangement can land around 1,700–2,000 QAR, a moderate studio lifestyle around 4,900–5,900 QAR, and a 1‑bed + car lifestyle often ranges 10,000–13,000 QAR+. Use our Qatar presets to estimate your potential savings.

Is Kuwait expensive for expats?

Kuwait City has higher rents in KWD terms but remember the currency is strong. A basic shared setup can be around 145–170 KWD monthly, a moderate studio lifestyle roughly 410–430 KWD, and a 1‑bed + car lifestyle around 890–970 KWD. The calculator converts all line items to KWD and totals them for clarity.

What’s the monthly cost of living in Bahrain?

Manama offers a range of options. A basic shared setup can be done around 155–170 BHD, a moderate studio lifestyle near 435–445 BHD, and a 1‑bed + car lifestyle about 980–1,010 BHD. Results vary with neighborhood and lifestyle habits—toggle the Bahrain preset to see details.

How much do I need to live in Muscat (Oman)?

Muscat can be more affordable in rent but you’ll likely need a car. A tight budget shared setup is around 125–130 OMR, a moderate studio lifestyle roughly 370–390 OMR, and a 1‑bed + car lifestyle near 805–840 OMR. Check the Oman preset to see the car and utilities assumptions.
